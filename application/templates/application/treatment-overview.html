Treatment Overview

Goals of Therapy
  • Remission of Symptoms (PHQ-9 < 5)
  • Improve function
  • Improve quality of life
  • Incorporate Psychotherapy
  • Prevent relapse
Medication Process Monitoring Week 1
  • Decrease in anxiety
  • Appetite and sleep pattern normalization
  • Assess side effects and encourage adherence
 Week 2 & 3
  • Increase in energy (may increase risk of suicide)
  • Improved concentration and memory
 Week 4-6
  • Improved mood
  • Decreased suicidal ideation
  • Assess sexual function
Duration of Treatment
  • 6-12 weeks of treatment is needed to stabilize depressive symptoms
  • Continue therapeutic dose for at least 4-9 months then may try to taper
  • Monitor for signs of relapse
  • Maintenance treatment may be needed for severe or recurrent episodes
Discontinuation of Antidepressants
  • Reduce dose by no more than 25% every week over the course of 4-8 weeks
  • If intolerable withdrawal symptoms occur, resume previous dose and slow the taper rate
Common Withdrawal Symptoms
  • Flu-like symptoms
  • Dizziness
  • Shock sensations
  • Malaise
  • Headache
  • Nausea
  • Lethargy
  • Abnormal dreams
  • Changes in mood, sleep, memory, and concetration
