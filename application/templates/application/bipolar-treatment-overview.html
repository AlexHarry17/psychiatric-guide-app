Bipolar Treatment Overview

Goals of Therapy

    Acute:

  • Control current episode
  • Reduce harm to self and others

    • Maintenance:

  • Prevent or minimize future episodes
  • Optimize medication adherence
  • Reduce drug adverse effects and drug interactions
  • Educate the patient and family about the disorder
  • Follow-up on a regular schedule
  • Optimize function and quality of life
General Approach to Treatment

    Pharmalogical:

  • Identify and manage secondary causes of mania such as antidepressants, drug abuse, alchohol abuse, hypothyroidism, or dementia
  • Initiate mood stabilizer
  • During acute episode, medications can be added (or increased) and then tapered once the patient is stablized
  • Do not use an antidepressent without a mood stabilizer
  • Antidepressants may destablize mood or cause a switch to mania/hypomania
  • Add an antipsychotic if psychosis is present

    • Non Pharmacological

  • Inform patient, family, and caregivers of self-help and support groupos
  • Address adequate nutrition, sleep, exercise, and stress reduction
  • Encourage psychotherapy and supportive counseling
  • Minimize nicotine and stop caffeeine intake 8 hours prior to bedtime
  • Document mood episodes in a daily mood chart
  • Treat comorbid substance use and abuse
Monitoring
  • Monitor patients ever 1-2 weeks for 6 weeks when starting new medications or switching therapies
  • Once stabilized, monitor patient every month for 3 months, then every 2-3 months
  • Monitor for depression using PHQ-9
  • Continually assess suicidality
  • Monitor medication adherence as this may be a reason for non-response and recurrent episodes
